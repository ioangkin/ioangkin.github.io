Approach

Contents origin

Most of the content have been sourced online, mainly from the official Wireshark website and wiki but also from third party sources. Complete reference list at the end of this document.

Additional content generated by experience gained after downloading and installing the application locally for testing the tutorial.

Style of writing

Although initially I chose a rather formal or technical language for writing the tutorial, during the process I realized that a more playful character would make it more attractive also due to the nature of the subject (as being popular with to-be hackers individuals). I even chose a rather provoking title with terms like hacking the network and spooking on others. In reality however, Wireshark is a rather harmless product, firstly because it can only be used to analyze data, rather than intervene and change things, and secondly its inability to actually show any readable content in packets anymore due to wide spread of encrypted protocols.

What went well, what didn't

The whole process worked well without many unexpected issues. There is a lot of work already done and at some point I even felt overwhelmed with the amount of information.

I enjoyed particularly attempting the tutorials steps on my network and exploring Wiresharks features.

Website submission

As part of the submission we are required to setup the tutorial on universitys hosting service. However, the service has recently been refurbished and changed location, however no communications have been released about the change yet. There has also been a modules tutorial on hosting a page on the new service, however this is not documented without notes in student-central and I regret that I did not complete the task. However, the produced document is an html page that can be hosted as long as I know how.

And what have learnt.

Communication encryptions is a very powerful tool in the hands of webmasters and users, especially following the revelations about governments comms surveillance programs, and is encouraging that it keeps on growing. I was even surprised reading articles from web masters on the troubleshooting disadvantages they have by not being able to read the packets of their SSL/HTTP networks, despite that the troubleshooting feedback can be achieved with other ways rather than spooking in the packets content and at the same time being able to protect their work and users form unauthorised eyes.

What most surprised me

The only part that I found not to be common, and I was surprised about it, is the lack of information about the increased inability to reads Wiresharks output with due to widespread use of HTTPS and encryption. With the current spread of secure connections is impossible to read the applications output and this change has not been communicated enough on the majority of online content. One reason however may be due to the quick advances in the subject; Even until recently several web servers were running only on HTTP protocol and since then not much information and training content has been updated yet.

What could improve

Although I am quite satisfied with the content and the quality of my tutorial, note that I have been writing tutorials before and I feel that I have gained some good skills at doing it, I would prefer if I was able to create some more attractive content, as of graphics and layout, instead of a rather simplistic and unimaginative presentation. Although I understand that design is not part of the criteria for the assignment, I believe that an attractive presentation can pass the message easier and have a greater impact to the reader.
